Getting Started
Connecting your Deriv account
1
Go to app.deriv.com/account/api-token
2
Create a token with Read + Trading information scopes
3
On the login screen, select Deriv API tab and paste your token
4
Your trades and balance sync automatically in real time
💡 Tip: Your token is saved in your browser — you won't need to paste it again next visit.
View historical data without connecting
Click View Only on the login screen to load the dashboard with pre-loaded historical Deriv trade data (Jan–Mar 2026) without connecting any account.
Switching between accounts
Use the Account bar just below the connection status bar to switch between:

• All — combined view of every account
• Deriv — your Deriv historical + live trades
• FTMO / The5ers — appear after importing files

Every tab shows its own independent stats, charts, and journal.
Importing MT5 Trades
How to export from MT5
1
Open MT5 and press Ctrl+T to open the Terminal
2
Click the History tab at the bottom
3
Right-click anywhere in the list → click All History
4
Right-click again → click Report → Save as HTML
5
Import the saved file into the dashboard
How to import into the dashboard
Option A — On login screen: Select MT5 Import tab, choose your broker (FTMO / The5ers / Other), then drop or click to select your file.

Option B — Already inside: Click the 📂 badge in the top header, select your broker, then drop your file.

The dashboard auto-detects the broker from the file content — so even if you select the wrong one, it will correct itself.
Supported file formats
• .HTML / .HTM — MT5 Report (recommended, most accurate)
• .CSV — MT5 Export to Spreadsheet
• .PDF — MT5 PDF report (trade rows must be present, not summary-only)

Warning: Note: PDF summary reports (showing only charts and stats) cannot be parsed. Use HTML or CSV for best results.
Supported prop firms / brokers
Automatically detected from file content:

• FTMO
• The5ers (Five Percent Online)
• MyFundedFX, E8 Funding, Topstep, FundedNext
• Any other MT5 broker — select Other and type the name
Removing an import
Click the 📂 badge in the header → scroll to Import History → click ✕ next to the import you want to remove. This deletes all trades from that broker.
Understanding Your Stats
Profit Factor
Gross Profit ÷ Gross Loss. A reading of 1.0 means breakeven.
• Below 1.0 — losing money overall
• 1.0 – 1.5 — marginal, needs improvement
• 1.5+ — passing FTMO / prop firm target
• 2.0+ — excellent performance
Win Rate
Percentage of trades that closed in profit. A low win rate is fine if your RRR is high. For example: 35% win rate with a 2.5 RRR is still profitable. You only need ~29% win rate to break even with a 2.5 RRR.
Average RRR (Risk:Reward Ratio)
Average win size ÷ average loss size. An RRR of 2.0 means your average win is twice your average loss. Higher is better — it means fewer wins are needed to be profitable.
Expectancy
The average amount you expect to make per trade. A positive expectancy means the strategy is profitable long-term. Calculated as: (Win Rate × Avg Win) − (Loss Rate × Avg Loss).
Equity Curve
Shows your running account balance over time. A smooth upward curve indicates consistent performance. Large dips indicate drawdown periods. The curve resets to the correct starting balance for each account (e.g. $5,000 for The5ers, $10,000 for Deriv).
Trading Journal
Creating a journal entry
Method 1: Click Journal tab → click "+ New Journal Entry"

Method 2: Go to History tab → click "+ Log" next to any trade row

Method 3: When a live trade closes, a popup appears asking if you want to log it — click Add Note
Account-specific journals
Each account has its own journal. When you're on the FTMO tab, only FTMO journal entries are shown and new entries are tagged as FTMO. Switch to The5ers and you see only The5ers journals. Select All to see every journal entry across all accounts.
Attaching chart screenshots
In the journal form, scroll to Chart Screenshots section. You can:

• Click the upload zone to select images
• Drag & drop images onto the zone
• Paste directly with Ctrl+V / Cmd+V

Click any saved chart image to open it in the fullscreen lightbox viewer. Use arrow keys to navigate multiple images.
Where is journal data stored?
All journal entries (including images) are saved in your browser's local storage. They persist across visits as long as you use the same browser. They are private to your device and never sent anywhere. Do not clear browser data or you will lose your entries.
Session rating & emotions
Rate your session quality with 1–5 stars. Tag your emotional state (Confident, Anxious, FOMO, Disciplined, etc.). Over time this helps identify which emotional states correlate with winning or losing trades.
Pro Tips
Keyboard shortcuts
Esc — Close any modal or lightbox
← → — Navigate between chart images in lightbox
Ctrl+V — Paste screenshot into journal form
Updating your imported trades
You can re-import a newer export file at any time. The dashboard automatically skips duplicates and only adds new trades. Your existing journal entries are never affected by re-importing.
Accessing from your phone
Bookmark this dashboard URL on any device for quick access. It works on mobile browsers. Note: journal entries and imports saved on your phone are stored separately from your computer — they won't sync between devices (each browser has its own local storage).
Myfxbook Live Sync
Connect your Myfxbook account for automatic trade sync from FTMO, The5ers and any MT5 prop firm.

Setup:
1. Create a free account at myfxbook.com
2. Add your MT5 accounts (Auto Update option — free)
3. Go to Live tab in this dashboard
4. Enter your Myfxbook email + password
5. Trades sync automatically every 10 minutes

⚡ Note: MT5 must be open on your computer for Myfxbook to receive new trades.
